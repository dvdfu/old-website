Drop is a simple libgdx game created to walkthrough some of the basic features of libgdx. My modifications to this game mark my first steps into libgdx and mobile game development in general.

Whether or not I stick to developing games in Java, I would like flexibility to a far greater depth than standard game engines. To me, part of making games is intensive programming and when I claim to have made a game, I want the claim to be greater than having simply provided its assets and assembling pre-built game engine functions. This is why I chose libgdx. I don’t know for how long I’ll be using it, but I hope to make at least one complete project before looking for other libraries/platforms.

Anyway, the initial game’s goal is to move a bucket underneath through endless stream of falling droplets as calming ambient rainfall plays in the background.

I’ve re-purposed the game to emulate the gameplay of Flappybird, though you can actually stand and collide with the top and bottom of the ‘pipes’. Also, colliding with their sides doesn’t kill you, instead accumulating “Shame points”.

The background music features Blackout City by Anamanaguchi.

I’m looking into improving the visuals and manipulating sprites before I look into the guts of the program.
