I’m going to go a bit more in depth about the gameplay mechanics of my new gem game.

Every stage of the game presents a different arrangement of blocks. The goal of each stage is to destroy (i.e. “mine”) every block with a gem in it to collect the gem. Every gem must be collected, none are to remain to pass a stage.

There are many ways to collect gems:

  • dump the block in water
  • place a block on gates (those black bars) and activate the gates by placing a block on its button
  • match neighbouring gem block edges
  • surround a block with 4 gems facing towards it, also destroying the center block
  • use a dynamite (unpictured)

As you can imagine, there are many strategies and many level possibilities. These above tools may help or hinder your level progress. I should make it clear that the footage above is just to test all of these features, though, real levels will usually have very few gems and involve a lot of clever thinking to collect them all.

I’m in the process of figuring out how to store level data. I could make the data human-readable: “Hannah and the Pirate Caves” did this on Neopets - their levels could be built in a text editor. But since this is an app and no one needs to or can see the level data in plain text, I’m considering something more abstract. Right now, all of the block’s true/false properties are converted to two-digit hex, but it’s a bit more complicated for objects like button-gate pairs and 2-way paths. I feel like once I know how to read/write level data I can make a lot of more presentable progress.
