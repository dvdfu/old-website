Managed to rip a Super Mario Bros. tileset, use Tiled to make a map out of it, and load/run it on libgdx. Without properly initializing and updating an OrthographicCamera, nothing is displayed properly. I don’t know the precise purpose of the OrthographicCamera yet besides mapping display/input coordinates, which would be useful when running the same program on multiple platforms i.e. mobile.

Otherwise, not too much coding done today, but I learned a lot about libgdx and have a more solid understanding of what it’s capable of and how I should approach it. Using libgdx is accepting that a lot of my work will go into the backend of the game, more precisely tuning the game’s engine so that it matches my needs. While it would be nice to focus mostly on frontend and pushing out as many games as possible, I don’t feel personally accomplished if I can’t say I didn’t work on making the game work. So libgdx is actually very well suited for me.

I also encountered a very nice blog/tutorial series that has a ton of information relevant to what I’m going to be working on.

My current goal is to just chip away at libgdx until I can make a new game template with ease. That level of familiarity will be really helpful. It’s a big time investment so I really hope I picked the right framework to go with.
